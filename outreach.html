Community Outreach

We believe STEM should reach further than our own pit. Here's how Cyclodials gives back to the community.

Upcoming Events

Coming Soon

FTC Pit Tours for FLL Kids

We plan to open up our pit to First LEGO League (FLL) teams from local elementary and middle schools, giving them an up-close look at what competing in FTC actually looks like. Students will get to see the robot in person, learn how it was designed and built, watch live demonstrations of our autonomous and driver-controlled modes, and talk directly with our team members about what goes into a full FTC season. The goal is to give younger students a real preview of what they can work toward as they grow in the FIRST program.

Coming Soon

STEM Robot Demo at Elementary & Middle Schools

We plan to visit elementary and middle schools in the Fremont area to run live robot demonstrations in classrooms and gymnasiums. Students will see the robot navigate autonomously, pick up game pieces, and respond to driver input — all while our team breaks down the engineering decisions behind each system. We'll cover the basics of mechanical design, software, and CAD in an accessible way, with the aim of sparking early interest in robotics and STEM for students who may never have seen a competition robot before.

Coming Soon

Beginner Robotics Workshop

We plan to host hands-on robotics workshops open to elementary and middle school students with zero prior experience. Participants will work through the fundamentals of building a simple robot chassis, wiring basic components, and writing their first lines of control code. The workshop will be structured so that every student walks away having built and driven something themselves — not just watched. Our goal is to lower the barrier to entry for students who are curious about robotics but don't know where to start.

Coming Soon

FLL Team Mentorship Program

We plan to establish an ongoing mentorship program for FLL teams at local elementary and middle schools. Our members will work directly with these younger teams throughout their season — helping them think through robot design, refine their autonomous programs, prepare for judging presentations, and develop the kind of strategic mindset that makes a difference at tournaments. We want to build a real pipeline in our community where students move up through FIRST with support from teams who have been through it before.
